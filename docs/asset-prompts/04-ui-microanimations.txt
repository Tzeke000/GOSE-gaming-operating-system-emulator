UI MICRO-ANIMATION REFERENCES — window + cursor motion language
These are REFERENCE clips for tuning the CSS/JS animations by eye (the real
implementations are code, not video). Generate at 1080p, dark background,
2-3 seconds each, slow motion welcome.

1. WINDOW OPEN — "A dark UI window materializes on an onyx desktop: it
   scales up from 92% to 100% over a quarter second while fading in, with
   a subtle cyan edge-glow that blooms once and settles. Smooth
   ease-out-expo motion, premium OS feel, no bounce."
2. WINDOW CLOSE — "The same window releases: scales down slightly while
   fading, its edge-glow collapsing into a thin line that winks out.
   Quick, decisive, calm — under 200ms feel."
3. WINDOW MINIMIZE (widget tier) — "The window folds toward the dock —
   scale + slight vertical squash along the travel path, leaving a brief
   light trail that fades. Playful but restrained."
4. CURSOR APPEAR/DISAPPEAR — "A small luminous pointer fades in and grows
   from 80% to 100% with a single soft pulse ring when the stick moves;
   after idle, it exhales — shrinking slightly while fading out over half
   a second." (matches the 5s-idle auto-hide behavior)
5. FOCUS HOP — "The blue focus glow jumps from one tile to a neighbor:
   the glow leads the jump like a drop of light, stretching slightly
   toward the target then snapping crisp onto it. 120ms."

NOTE: motion vocabulary must stay consistent: ease-out, sub-250ms,
glow-leads-motion, nothing bounces. Reduce-motion users get opacity-only
versions — every one of these needs that fallback when implemented.
